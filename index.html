Game Features

Hidden Plant

New Plant Sour Shot

Description

Sour Shot is an offensive-type plant that attacks by launching acidic lemon-shaped projectiles that splash in multiple directions upon hitting a target. Each shot bounces and delivers a splash effect to nearby zombies, making it highly effective against crowds. Its projectiles also slow down affected zombies for a few seconds. With a moderate cooldown and relatively low sun cost, Sour Shot is ideal for early-game deployment. The plant has strong synergy with control-type plants due to its ability to maintain attack distance. The acidic effect of its projectiles can also penetrate light defenses like cone and bucket zombies more quickly. When fertilized, Sour Shot unleashes a large lemon rain across an entire row, dealing massive damage and briefly stunning zombies. Its ability to hit multiple zombies at once makes it a reliable crowd control weapon. While not very effective against heavy metal-type zombies like Gargantuars, Sour Shot can significantly slow their movement. It grows quickly and requires minimal space to activate its attacks. Its attack animation is unique, featuring striking green-yellow acid splashes. In long-term defense strategies, Sour Shot can save many support plants thanks to its flexible role. It is also resistant to weather effects such as fog or snow, making it dependable in almost any battlefield. Flying-type zombies are immune to its splash effect, requiring additional anti-air plants. Nonetheless, its combination of area damage, slowing effect, and powerful synergy with plant food makes Sour Shot a strong contender to become a player favorite in upcoming content.

Upcoming Content

New Plant Brainstem

Description

Brainstem is a unique support plant that manipulates zombie behavior using psionic waves emitted from its main stem. When planted, Brainstem releases a purple aura that causes nearby zombies to move slower and become easier to control. Its main effect is to confuse zombies, allowing them to attack each other randomly for a few seconds. This plant is highly effective for disrupting zombie formations and creating chaos on the enemy front line. Although it doesn't attack directly, Brainstem plays a major role in survival strategy. Zombies affected by its aura also take additional damage from other plant attacks. The plant has a 3x3 area of effect around it, making it most effective when placed at the center of the lawn. When fertilized, Brainstem emits a powerful wave that stuns all zombies on screen for several seconds. This stun effect also temporarily disables zombies' special abilities. Brainstem has a slow planting time and a medium sun cost, balanced with its tactical potential. Its effects do not apply to boss zombies, but still affect elite or shielded ones. It is also immune to mental attacks like those from hypno-zombies, making it resilient in psychological battles. Visually, Brainstem stands out with a glowing brain and pulsing roots that resemble living nerves. This plant pairs well with high-damage plants to maximize its disruption effect. In survival or arena modes, Brainstem can be the key to turning the tide when waves of zombies attack relentlessly. With its ability to influence enemy minds, Brainstem is set to become one of the most strategic support plants in the upcoming Plants vs Zombies 2 content.

Upcoming Content

New Plant Lemon-Aid

Description

Lemon-Aid is a healing and protective plant that combines supportive functions with a refreshing area effect. When planted, Lemon-Aid automatically sprays lemon mist forward and sideways, periodically healing nearby plants within its range. Its healing effect increases with level, allowing near-destroyed plants to return to optimal condition. In addition to healing, Lemon-Aid provides a "refresh" effect that accelerates the recharge time of surrounding plants. With a low to medium sun cost, Lemon-Aid is best placed in the second row to support frontline defenses. When fertilized, this plant releases a large citrus wave that heals all plants on the screen and grants them a temporary shield. Zombies affected by the lemon mist suffer a weakened state, reducing their attack power for a few seconds. Lemon-Aid pairs perfectly with frontline plants like Wall-nut or Endurian to extend their durability. Its visual animation is cheerful, featuring light yellow lemon bubbles that glow softly. This plant is also immune to negative weather effects like ice and poison, making it an ideal choice for extreme-themed levels. Lemon-Aid has a fairly fast cooldown, allowing it to be used multiple times in a single session. While it does not attack zombies directly, its healing and strategic boosting effects make it a central synergy plant. In Arena and Battlez modes, Lemon-Aid can be the difference between victory and defeat with its ability to maintain formation stability. Its healing effect also works on summon-type plants like Grave Buster and Potato Mine. With its multifunctional role and refreshing appearance, Lemon-Aid is a strong candidate to become a favorite support plant in the upcoming Plants vs Zombies 2 update.

Upcoming Content

New Plant Rose

Description

Rose is a magic-type plant capable of manipulating time and transforming zombies into harmless forms. Every few seconds, Rose fires pink magical petals that have a chance to turn regular zombies into cute sheep that walk slowly and cannot attack. Her attack also slows down zombie movement speed, making her ideal for controlling the pace of battle. This transfiguration effect is temporary, but enough to delay and disrupt incoming zombie waves. Rose is most effective when placed in the middle row, as her attacks curve diagonally. When given Plant Food, she casts a powerful area spell that slows all zombies on screen and instantly transforms three random zombies into sheep. While she doesn't deal high damage, her control ability is exceptional and pairs well with offensive plants. Boss zombies or those with heavy armor are immune to transformation, but still affected by her slowing effect. Rose has a moderate cooldown and a reasonably affordable sun cost for a magic plant of her tier. She has an elegant appearance with a glowing petal crown and sparkling star effects when attacking. She's especially useful in strategy and puzzle levels that require precise timing and crowd management. Rose also empowers nearby magic plants, granting a small passive buff within a 3x3 radius. The visual effect of zombies turning into sheep is very satisfying and adds a whimsical charm to the gameplay. In the world of Plants vs Zombies 2, Rose brings a magical touch and advanced strategic value for experienced players.
